What is SIG?

The Sustainable Investment Group at UC San Diego (SIG) is a student-run growth investment organization that conducts research and invests in stocks of companies in industry sectors conducive to sustainability to achieve high returns. We teach students various skills such as modeling, researching, industry analysis, and presentation both broadly and through the unique lens of sustainability, giving students experience in sustainability, finance, and counseling.

Club Structure

Our club is broken down into 5 main committees:

  1. Equity Research
  2. Sustainability
  3. Quantitative Analysis
  4. Communications, Outreach, & Marketing
  5. Research & Development
ALL COMMITTEES ARE RECRUITING!

Equity Research

As an analyst on the Equity Research Committee you can expect to:

  • Contribute research and analysis of publicly traded companies to make accurate investment decisions

  • Using different valuation techniques:
    • Discounted Cash Flow Model
    • Comparable Companies Analysis
  • Analyzing financial statements
  • Keeping up with industry trends and the economic climate
  • Incorporating ESG factors into our investment decisions

Our projects include:

  • Stock Pitch (New/Returning Members)
    • Support a team of Sustainability and Quant analysts to pitch a stock
  • Equity Performance Reports (New/Returning Members)
    • Work with peers to research and report on equities that should be integrated into an ESG focused portfolio
  • Macroeconomic Indicator Reports (Returning Members)
    • Report and educate on the health of the economy based on qualitative and quantitative data

Sustainability

As a new analyst on the Sustainability Committee you can expect to:

  • Conduct ESG research and analysis of companies to determine sustainability and long-term impact
    • Research includes (but not limited to) looking at: ESG analysis, ESG scoring, green-washing, carbon accounting, DCF model w/ ESG considerations
    • Keeping updates on the economic climate and real-world news and events
    • Incorporating ESG into consultations and analyses of companies
  • Work with other committees and participate in the quarterly Stock Pitch Competition

Returning analysts can expect to:

  • Have the opportunity to work on a long-term project combining sustainability with finance
    • Analysts can decide research topics
    • Ex.: Writing (and hopefully publishing) research reports/presentations related to company analyses!

Quantitative Analysis

New analysts develop and apply models to forecast and understand a company's financials

  • Sample of models: ARCH, GARCH, ARIMA, Linear and Multiple Regression, ESG-CAPM.
  • Understanding Macro/Micro trends and how it affects firm behaviour.
  • Understanding the stock market and general market behaviour.
  • We use Python and standard packages.
  • No prior technical experience is required!

Returning analysts can continue to partake in the stock pitch competition or present a project at our quarterly Quantathon.

What is Quantathon? - A quarterly exposition of projects developed by our members

  • Past Projects: Formula 1 race performance, Option pricing calculators, Correlation analysis with neural networks, Internal SIG tools for automated DCF valuations
  • Judges from industry professionals as well as UCSD professors

Additionally we also compete in data-thons! Our last one was UCLA's ASA Datafest in Spring - We sent a team of 5 members to compete on behalf of SIG and to represent UCSD

Communications & Marketing

In the Communications & Marketing Committee, you can expect to:

  • Organize and host internal events (socials, fundraisers, celebrations)
  • Plan & promote external events
    • competitions and conferences in collaboration with UCSD academic departments and UC Investments
    • cross-club socials/competitions with other student-run UCSD organizations
  • Create and regulate marketing materials
  • Manage social media presence and publicity

Committee Mission:

  • Improve membership experience by creating meaningful opportunities to network and grow professionally together
  • Members will gain hands-on experience in event planning, creative marketing, as well as building and maintaining strong professional relationships
  • We aim to provide the space to ideate and cultivate a strong org culture

Research & Development

As a member of the R&D Committee you can expect to:

  • Collaborate with Finance and Communications & Marketing to help raise capital for the fund.
  • Assist in constructing investment philosophies and frameworks that adhere to SIG's mission.
  • Develop portfolio optimization strategies and other financial models that seek to integrate ESG considerations into our investment decision-making.
  • Develop and publish a working portfolio optimizer which will be used to make real trades
